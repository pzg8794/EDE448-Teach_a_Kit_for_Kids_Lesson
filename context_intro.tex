\reviewsection{Lesson Context and Purpose}

I used OAR's Kit for Kids materials because they translate autism acceptance into language elementary students can understand. The core story, \textit{What's Up with Nick?}, presents an autistic student through a classmate's perspective, while the Friendship Tip Sheets turn broad ideas such as kindness and acceptance into concrete peer actions \citep{oar2026kit,oar2026nick,oar2026tips}.

I emphasized that autism is one way a brain can work and that autistic students may communicate, move, learn, regulate, or experience the environment in many different ways. The lesson therefore focused on respectful peer actions rather than asking students to identify, explain, or judge another person's diagnosis.
